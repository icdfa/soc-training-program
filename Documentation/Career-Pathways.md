
# SOC Training Program - Career Pathways

## Introduction

This document outlines the potential career paths for graduates of the SOC Training Program. The skills and knowledge gained in this program will prepare you for a variety of roles in the cybersecurity industry.

## Entry-Level Roles

*   **SOC Analyst (Tier 1):** This is the most common entry-level role for graduates of this program. Tier 1 analysts are responsible for monitoring security alerts, performing initial triage, and escalating incidents to Tier 2 analysts.
*   **Cybersecurity Analyst:** This is a more general entry-level role that may involve a variety of tasks, such as vulnerability scanning, security awareness training, and policy development.

## Mid-Level Roles

*   **SOC Analyst (Tier 2):** Tier 2 analysts are responsible for in-depth investigation of security incidents, malware analysis, and incident response.
*   **Incident Responder:** Incident responders are responsible for responding to and recovering from security incidents.
*   **Threat Hunter:** Threat hunters proactively search for threats in an enterprise environment.

## Senior-Level Roles

*   **SOC Analyst (Tier 3):** Tier 3 analysts are the most senior analysts in the SOC. They are responsible for advanced threat hunting, malware reverse engineering, and mentoring junior analysts.
*   **SOC Manager:** The SOC manager is responsible for overseeing the day-to-day operations of the SOC.
*   **Security Architect:** Security architects are responsible for designing and implementing secure network and system architectures.
