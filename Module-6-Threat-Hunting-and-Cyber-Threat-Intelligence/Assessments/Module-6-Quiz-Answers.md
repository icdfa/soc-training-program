_# Module 6 Quiz: Threat Hunting & Cyber Threat Intelligence - Answers

1. **What is the primary purpose of threat hunting?**
   - Threat hunting is the proactive search for cyber threats that are lurking undetected in a network. It is a continuous process of looking for signs of compromise, such as unusual network traffic, unauthorized access, and malicious activity.

2. **What is the difference between threat hunting and incident response?**
   - Threat hunting is a proactive process of searching for threats, while incident response is a reactive process of responding to a security incident that has already occurred. Threat hunting is about finding the unknown unknowns, while incident response is about dealing with the known unknowns.

3. **What is the purpose of the MITRE ATT&CK framework?**
   - The MITRE ATT&CK framework is a globally-accessible knowledge base of adversary tactics and techniques based on real-world observations. It is used as a foundation for the development of specific threat models and methodologies in the private sector, in government, and in the cybersecurity product and service community.

4. **What is the purpose of a threat intelligence platform (TIP)?**
   - A threat intelligence platform (TIP) is a technology solution that collects, aggregates, and organizes threat intelligence from multiple sources. It provides a single pane of glass for security analysts to view and analyze threat data, and to take action to mitigate threats.

5. **What is the difference between strategic, operational, and tactical threat intelligence?**
   - Strategic threat intelligence is high-level information that is used to inform business decisions. It is typically used by executives and other senior leaders. Operational threat intelligence is more technical information that is used to inform security operations. It is typically used by security managers and incident responders. Tactical threat intelligence is highly technical information that is used to inform security controls. It is typically used by security analysts and engineers.
